The 2024 Hungarian Grand Prix, held on July 21, 2024, at the Hungaroring, was a landmark race for McLaren, resulting in a 1-2 finish and the maiden Formula 1 victory for Oscar Piastri.

Winner: Oscar Piastri (McLaren)
Second Place: Lando Norris (McLaren)
Third Place: Lewis Hamilton (Mercedes)

The Start: McLaren locked out the front row with Lando Norris on pole and Oscar Piastri in second. At the start, Piastri got a better launch and took the lead at Turn 1. Max Verstappen, starting third, went off-track while challenging for the lead and rejoined ahead of Norris. Following a directive from his team, Verstappen later allowed Norris to pass him to retake second place.

McLaren Strategy Drama: The race is largely remembered for the internal tension regarding team orders. McLaren brought Norris in for his final pit stop before Piastri to cover off Lewis Hamilton, which inadvertently put Norris in the lead. The team subsequently instructed Norris to relinquish the position back to Piastri. After several laps of radio conversation, Norris slowed down on lap 68 to allow Piastri to retake the lead, which he held until the finish.

Verstappen's Difficult Day: Championship leader Max Verstappen struggled with the pace of his Red Bull relative to the McLarens and experienced increasing frustration on the team radio throughout the race. His day was further complicated by a late-race incident while attempting to overtake Lewis Hamilton for third place; the two cars made contact, causing Verstappen to go off-track and finish fifth.
