=== WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP TITLE HOLDERS ON THE 2025 GRID ===

There are 4 drivers on the current 2025 grid who have won the World Drivers' Championship:

1. Lewis Hamilton — 7 World Championships (2008, 2014, 2015, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020)
   Team: Ferrari (2025–present), previously Mercedes (2013–2024), McLaren (2007–2012)
   Career wins: 105 (as of end 2024)
   Nationality: British
   Notes: Joint record holder with Michael Schumacher for most championships (7). Most race wins in F1 history.

2. Max Verstappen — 4 World Championships (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024)
   Team: Red Bull Racing (2016–present), previously Toro Rosso (2015–2016)
   Career wins: 63 (as of end 2024)
   Nationality: Dutch
   Notes: Won his first title in 2021 in a controversial final-lap battle with Hamilton in Abu Dhabi. Dominated 2022 and 2023 with record-breaking win streaks.

3. Fernando Alonso — 2 World Championships (2005, 2006)
   Team: Aston Martin (2023–present), previously Alpine/Renault, Ferrari, McLaren, Minardi
   Career wins: 32
   Nationality: Spanish
   Notes: Youngest world champion at time of first title (24 years old). One of the most experienced drivers in F1 history.

4. Lando Norris — 1 World Championship (2025)
   Team: McLaren (2019–present)
   Career wins: 11 (as of end 2025 season)
   Nationality: British
   Notes: Won his maiden championship in 2025, becoming McLaren's first drivers' champion since Lewis Hamilton in 2008. His first race win came at the 2024 Miami Grand Prix.

COMBINED TOTAL: 4 champions on the 2025 grid with 14 championships combined (7 + 4 + 2 + 1 = 14).
